My name is Ammon Shepherd

I was born in Mesa, Arizona. I served a mission to Hamburg, Germany in the late 90's. I married Jessica in 2000. We have six kids, three boys and three girls. After graduating from Arizona State University with a Bachelors of Arts in German and one in History, my wife and two children moved to Virginia so I could do graduate work at George Mason University. My greatest accomplishment while there was adding four more children to our family. I also finished an MA in History and ended the Ph.D. program just short of successfully completing. I would say I'm ABD, but I actually did write a dissertation, but it was not acceptable before I ran out of time to complete it. For the curious, it is available here: https://ammonshepherd.github.io/nazitunnels-archive/dissertation.htmlLinks to an external site..

While working on degrees in history I kept my family fed and sheltered by working full-time in the IT field. I have worked as a desktop support specialist, freelance web developer, webmaster, systems administrator, Associate Director of Technology, Digital Humanities Developer, dev ops, and Makerspace manager (which means I get to learn, teach and use about 3D printers, electronics, sewing machines, button makers, and just about any other fun technology I can think of).

I love serving in the Church, and usually get to work with the youth. I have been Young Mens president, Elders Quorum president, Sunday School president (twice), early morning Seminary teacher (for 5 years), Bishop, Stake Institute teacher, and Stake YM Presidency. I am truly humbled to see the power of Jesus Christ's Atonement work in people's lives when they humble themselves and turn toward Him.

I love learning and helping others learn and understand technology. I'm excited to share this learning adventure with you!
